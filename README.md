# Tiny Planet Tow Co. Prototype

A small playable browser prototype for the Tiny Planet Tow Truck concept.

Open `index.html` in a browser to play.

The game opens with a short how-to-play briefing. Click `Start route` to begin.

Controls:

- `WASD` or arrow keys: move the tow ship
- `Space` or click: attach/release the tow cable
- `Q` / `E`: shorten or lengthen the cable
- `F`: fire the blaster
- `R`: reset the route
- Mobile: use the left joystick to steer, then use Hook, Fire, In, and Out buttons
- Mission HUD: fades into a small mission tab during play; click or tap it to expand

Prototype goal:

Navigate in from deep space, tow the highlighted object, and deliver it to the matching load zone. Deliver all five jobs to finish the route.

Challenge layer:

- Finish jobs quickly for time bonuses
- Keep cargo damage low for clean-delivery bonuses
- Avoid asteroids, debris fields, UFO traffic, orbiting debris, and hard planet impacts
- Shoot hazards to clear your path, but each destroyed object adds two smaller hazards back into play
- Each completed tow earns a 1-3 star rating
- Use the bottom-right compass to find the blue load zone when you drift away
- The compass points to the tow target until the correct cargo is attached, then switches to the load zone
- Spend credits between jobs on hook range, line strength, handling, and ship paint
- Reinforced line upgrades increase real line integrity, letting the tow cable survive more hits
- Meteor showers can knock the ship, damage cargo, or snap the tow line
- Debris or meteors crossing the tow cable can break it
- Different cargo jobs use different colored load zones
- Cargo reacts with short personality lines while being towed, hit, or delivered
- Emergency events can trigger meteor walls, UFO convoys, magnetic squalls, or load-zone blackouts
- Sound effects and light generated background music start after the first key press or click
- Desktop and mobile browser testing are both supported from the same GitHub Pages link
- Mission and status panels auto-collapse during play so the route stays visible
